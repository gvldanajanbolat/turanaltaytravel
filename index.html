The Sacred Altai & Tavan Bogd

The Altai Mountains are not just a range; they are the "Golden Pillars" of the world. Standing at the junction of Mongolia, Kazakhstan, Russia, and China, the Altai represents the rawest form of Earth's majesty. Our expeditions focus on the Tavan Bogd (Five Holy Peaks), home to the highest points in Mongolia. The most prominent is Khuiten Peak, reaching 4,374 meters. This region houses the massive Potanin Glacier, a sprawling river of ice that has shaped the nomadic spirit for eons. To stand here is to stand at the roof of the Central Asian world, where the air is pure and the silence is eternal.

Master of the Skies: Golden Eagle

In the high valleys of Altai, the bond between man and bird is a sacred pact. Hunting with Golden Eagles is an ancient art form that has survived for over 4,000 years. We offer you exclusive access to the 'Burkitshi' (Eagle Hunters). You will not just watch; you will live in their yurts, learn the call of the bird, and witness the incredible synchronization as the eagle dives from the mountain peaks at speeds exceeding 240 km/h. It is a spiritual dialogue between the nomad and the king of birds.

The 5 Great Chronicles of Altai

UNESCO World Heritage: Petroglyphs

Dating back 12,000 years, the rock art complexes of the Mongolian Altai provide the most complete record of human development in Central Asia. These carvings depict the transition from hunting to herding and contain the earliest known representations of nomadic chariots and sun-headed deities, carved with precision into the dark desert varnish of the stones.

Mystical Deer Stones

Rising like monoliths from the steppe, these Bronze Age stones are masterpieces of early art. They are carved with stylized "flying deer" with antlers reaching toward the sky. Scholars believe these stones represent great warriors transformed into celestial deer, serving as conduits between the earthly world and the divine heavens.

Scythian Pazyryk Secrets

The Iron Age Scythians were the undisputed masters of the steppe. The Altai permafrost has miraculously preserved their tombs, revealing incredible treasures: the world's oldest knotted wool carpet, sophisticated gold jewelry, and even tattooed remains. Our tour explores the sacred burial grounds where the "Ice Maiden" once rested.

The Guardians: Turkic Balbals

The Altai is the cradle of the Turkic Khaganates. Scattered across the valleys are 'Balbals'—stone statues carved in the likeness of honored heroes. Each statue holds a ritual cup, symbolizing their eternal presence at the great feast of the ancestors. They stand as silent guardians of the borders and the history of our people.

Royal Khirigsuur Mounds

These massive stone burial mounds, often surrounded by satellite stone circles, are the pyramids of the Altai. Built for elite leaders, they align with astronomical events. The sheer scale of the stone-work reflects a highly organized society that revered their leaders as gods who commanded the winds and the stars.

The Bactrian Camel Caravan

The two-humped Bactrian camel is the true "Ship of the Altai Desert." These majestic creatures are essential for navigating the high-altitude passes. Our camel trekking modules allow you to experience the rhythmic pace of the ancient Silk Road. Each camel can carry up to 250kg of equipment, allowing us to venture into the "No-Man's Land" where vehicles cannot reach. It is the most authentic way to connect with the landscape.
