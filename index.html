THE GOLDEN HERITAGE

1. Ancient Rock Art

Bayan-Olgii is home to prehistoric rock art dating from the Neolithic to the Bronze Age. Recognized as UNESCO World Heritage, these carvings depict hunting strategies, ancient chariots, and diverse wildlife.

2. Mysterious Deer Stones

Dating back to 1500–500 BC, these stones feature 'flying deer' motifs. They served as spirit guides for nomadic warriors, marking sacred burial and ritual sites.

3. Pazyryk Burial Mounds

Over 362 burial mounds documented. These Iron Age kurgans are world-renowned for preserving organic artifacts and even tattoos in permafrost for 2,000 years.

4. Turkic Ritual Legacy

Featuring 255 ritual complexes and 888 ancient graves. The stone 'Balbals' honor ancestors and warriors from the 6th-8th century Turkic Khaganate.

5. Khirigsuur Monuments

Massive stone structures found throughout the province. These Bronze Age monuments represent advanced communal efforts and astronomical alignment.
